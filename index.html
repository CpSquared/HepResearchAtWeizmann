Special Seminar More Particle Physics Endeavors at the University at Albany SUNY Department of Physics

Speaker: Matthew Szydagis.

Affiliation: Associate Professor at the University at Albany, State University of New York, USA. Homepage.

Seminar starts at: 13:00, Israel time.

Refreshments served at: 12:30, Israel time.

Date: 14 January, Wednesday.

Venue: Physics library.

Abstract: In this talk, I will discuss my efforts together with my colleague Prof. Cecilia Levy on both LZ-related and unrelated initiatives. In the first half, I will continue discussing the Noble Element Simulation Technique (NEST) from my first talk. This gold-standard sim, now featured in the Particle Data Group (PDG) review, describes light and charge yields of different types of energy depositions in liquid and gas Xe and Ar as functions of energy and electric fields, along with fluctuations, and temporal pulse structures, at an unprecedented degree of precision. NEST’s semi-empirical approach, which grew out of LZ predecessor LUX, has demonstrated incredible postdictive/predictive powers for many other experiments, including ZEPLIN, XENON, and PandaX for calibration and signal-background discrimination, namely, the separation of nuclear recoils from electron recoils. NEST's focus is still the traditional WIMP, however; to search for sub-GeV-mass dark matter, we have developed a new particle detection tech, i.e., the snowball chamber, the topic of the second half. It relies on supercooled water only freezing due to nuclear recoil. Like the bubble/cloud chambers before it, the snowball chamber has incredible promise.

Group meeting - Gilad Perez The Strong CP Problem and a U(1)-Embedded Hiller–Schmaltz Framework for Ultralight Dark Matter and CKM Oscillations.

Speaker: Ilay bar.

Affiliation: Ph.D. student with Prof. Gilad Perez, at Weizmann Institute of Science, Israel.

Seminar starts at: 13:00, Israel time.

Refreshments served at: 12:30, Israel time.

Date: 21 January, Wednesday.

Venue: Physics library.

Zoom link: Zoom (TBA).

Abstract: The nature of the strong CP problem remains the subject of ongoing discussion: while the observed smallness of θˉ is often taken to indicate new physics, it is still worth examining whether this interpretation is strictly required. After reviewing the status of the problem and some of the proposed solutions, I will present a model based on the Hiller–Schmaltz (HS) mechanism in which CP is embedded into a continuous U(1) symmetry. In this framework, the HS mechanism naturally yields an ultralight dark-matter (ULDM) candidate in the form of a Goldstone boson, together with its full supermultiplet that needs to decouple at low energies. The resulting oscillatory ULDM field induces time-dependent modifications of the CKM flavor parameters. I will discuss the structure of the model, the origin of the Goldstone mode, and the phenomenological consequences for quark mixing, including potential observational signatures.

Joint AMOS-DPPA seminar Dark Matter Recoherence

Speaker: Chaitanya Paranjape. (Chai)

Affiliation: Ph.D. student with Prof. Gilad Perez, at Weizmann Institute of Science, Israel.

Seminar starts at: 13:00, Israel time.

Refreshments served at: 12:30, Israel time.

Date: 16 February, Monday.

Venue: Physics library.

Zoom link: Zoom (TBA).

Abstract: The local solar gravitational potential forms a basin for ultralight dark matter (ULDM), with discrete energy levels. Even if barely populated, it introduces a new characteristic timescale in DM dynamics. This necessitates a generalization of the notion of coherence time. We find that, at long times, the phenomenon of recoherence emerges, whereby a subcomponent of ULDM exhibits a formally divergent coherence time. The fact that this generalized coherence time can significantly exceed the naive estimate implies an enhanced sensitivity for dark matter searches that accumulate data over extended observation periods. Based on the work, arXiv:2601.00955.
