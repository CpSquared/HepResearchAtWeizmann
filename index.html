Group meeting - Gilad Perez Powerspectrum and Bispectrum analysis of quadratically coupled ULDM.

Speaker: Preetham Kumar Adepu

Affiliation: M.Sc. student with Prof. Gilad Perez, at Weizmann Institute of Science, Israel.

Seminar starts at: 13:00, Israel time.

Refreshments served at: 12:30, Israel time.

Date: May 20, Wednesday.

Venue: Physics Weissman Aquarium.

Zoom link: Zoom (TBA).

Abstract: Nuclear clocks are emerging as precision probes in the search for ultralight dark matter, providing a sensitive platform to study tiny time-dependent variations of Standard Model parameters. Hence, statistical tools become essential for identifying possible fluctuations from the nuclear clock measurements. This seminar focuses on the power spectrum and bispectrum of the quadratic operator [\delta\theta^2(t)=\theta^2(t)-\langle \theta^2\rangle\] for ultralight dark matter with a Maxwellian velocity distribution. The power spectrum characterizes the two-point structure of the stochastic signal, including both the harmonic region near \(2m\) and the low-frequency component of width (\sim m\sigma^2\). The bispectrum extends this analysis to three-point correlations. Together, these observables provide a systematic framework for studying stochastic signatures relevant to future nuclear clock searches.

Hep-ph | Hep-ex | Seminar First model-independent measurement of the Xe136 two-neutrino double-beta (2νββ) decay spectrum.

Speaker: Barbara Paetsch.

Affiliation: Ph.D. student with Prof. Rani Budnik, at Weizmann Institute of Science, Israel.

Seminar starts at: 13:00, Israel time.

Refreshments served at: 12:30, Israel time.

Date: June 8, Monday.

Venue: Physics library.

Zoom link: Zoom (TBA).

Abstract: The two-neutrino double beta decay (2νββ) of Xe136 is a rare second-order weak process and one of the science channels studied by the XENONnT experiment at LNGS (Gran Sasso, Italy). The summed electron energy spectrum encodes nuclear structure information through the relevant nuclear matrix elements and may also reveal beyond-the-Standard-Model effects, such as sterile or self-interacting neutrinos, through distortions of the spectral shape. Using data from XENONnT’s first science run, we perform the first model-independent measurement of the Xe136 2νββ spectral shape down to a few keV. In this talk, I will briefly introduce the physics motivation and the XENONnT detector, and then discuss the analysis strategy and resulting measurement.

Hep-ph | Hep-ex | Seminar Coherent Elastic Neutrino-Nucleus Scattering (CEνNS).

Speaker: Dr. Olga Razuvaev.

Affiliation: PostDoc with Prof. Rani Budnik, at Weizmann Institute of Science, Israel.

Seminar starts at: 13:00, Israel time.

Refreshments served at: 12:30, Israel time.

Date: June 29, Monday.

Venue: Physics library.

Zoom link: Zoom (TBA).

Abstract: Coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CEνNS) is a process predicted by the Standard Model more than fifty years ago, but it was only observed experimentally for the first time in 2017 by the COHERENT collaboration. This process is dominant for low-energy neutrinos. Nowadays there are many experiments around the world dedicated to studying CEνNS with different target materials and neutrino sources. The goal of these studies is not so much to confirm the Standard Model, but rather to search for physics beyond it. The process is also a key to understanding supernova explosions and the limitations imposed on dark matter searches. This seminar provides an overview of the CEvNS studies and their successes.
